## Subscriber Psychology by Tier

### NEW Subscribers
**What they want:** Proof the creator is real, attention, testing the waters
**Your job:** Make first sale, establish rapport, qualify spending potential

```
Key moves:
- Warm welcome (make them feel special for subscribing)
- Light qualification ("what brought you here?")
- Low-friction first offer
- Plant seeds for relationship
```

### LOW Tier ($1-100 lifetime)
**What they want:** Value, proof content is worth it
**Your job:** Convert to repeat buyer, identify if upgradeable

```
Key moves:
- Direct approach works fine
- Focus on value ("this is my best video")
- Don't over-invest time if they don't respond
- Occasional check-ins to reactivate
```

### MEDIUM Tier ($100-500 lifetime)
**What they want:** Consistency, feeling of connection
**Your job:** Build habit, deepen relationship, upsell

```
Key moves:
- Remember things about them
- Personalized offers
- Mix content sales with genuine conversation
- Start GFE elements if they respond
```

### HIGH Tier ($500-2000 lifetime)
**What they want:** Exclusivity, special treatment, emotional connection
**Your job:** Full relationship mode, custom content, maximize LTV

```
Key moves:
- Full GFE approach
- "Just for you" content
- Regular check-ins even without sales intent
- Remember birthdays, details, stories
- Make them feel like boyfriend material
```

### WHALE Tier ($2000+ lifetime)
**What they want:** Deep connection, ultimate exclusivity, feeling irreplaceable
**Your job:** Premium relationship, maximum personalization, protect at all costs

```
Key moves:
- Top priority response time
- Never make them feel like "just another sub"
- Exclusive content no one else gets
- Real-feeling conversations
- They should feel like they're actually dating you
```

## Balancing Romance and Revenue

The tension: You need to make money, but pushiness kills relationships.

### The Balance
- 70% relationship building, 30% direct selling
- Never more than 2 pitches per conversation
- Always check in without selling sometimes
- After they buy, don't immediately sell more
- Let them come to you sometimes

### Reading the Room
**Signs to sell:**
- They're engaged and chatty
- They mentioned having money
- They're in a good mood
- They brought up wanting content

**Signs to chill:**
- Short responses
- Said they're broke/busy
- Just bought something
- Seem distracted/off

## Metrics That Matter

1. **Response rate** - Are they engaging?
2. **Average time to sale** - How long from first message to purchase?
3. **Repeat purchase rate** - Are they coming back?
4. **Tier progression** - Are subs moving up?
5. **LTV by approach** - Which relationship style works best?
